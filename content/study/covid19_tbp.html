Published: 15 August 2020

The Big Picture

Traffic: Despite the continuous increase since mid-April 2020, there were a total of 3.47 million fewer flights in the EUROCONTROL Member States compared to the first seven months in 2019 (-54.5% vs. 2019). Focusing on the period May to July, traffic in 2020 was even -74.7% below 2019 levels.
More detailed information on the COVID-19 impact by State, Air Navigation Service Provider, ACC or airport

Charges: As a consequence of the unprecedented reduction of traffic, revenues from en-route charges between January and July 2020 were -57.0% lower than planned which corresponds to a 2.67 billion Euro loss in the first seven months of 2020.
Explore revenues by Charging Zone

Delays: As can be expected, ATFM en-route delays dropped to zero following the dramatic reduction in traffic in mid-April. Compared to the unacceptably high delays observed in 2019, en-route delays decreased by 84.6% which corresponds to 8.77 million minutes less delay.
See more information on ATFM delays by entity and delays cause

Road to recovery

The rate of recovery is in line with the EUROCONTROL “Coordinated Measures” Scenario which envisaged around 38% of the 2019 traffic level by the end of July.

The latest European NOP 2020 Recovery Plan expects to reach close to 60% of the 2019 traffic levels on peak days in September. However, this outlook is highly dependent on the evolution of State restrictions.
